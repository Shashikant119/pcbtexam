Please read the instructions carefully

General Instructions:
  1. Total duration of examination : 150 minutes (Part-A - 90 mins and Part-B - 60 mins)
  2. Number of questions:175 (Part-A – 100 Questions, Part-B – 75 Questions)
  3. All questions are of objective type. Four options are given to each question out of which only one will be the correct answer.
  4. There will be negative marking for wrong answers. 1/3rd mark will be deducted for every wrong answer.
  5. Click on Save and Next button after answering every question to save your answer. Otherwise your answer will not be saved.
  6. Language: : You can view the question paper in English, Hindi and the language in which you had appeared in the 1st stage CBT for ALP and Technicians. The instructions and the questions shall be displayed in the language chosen by you from the drop down language list available at the bottom of the 'other important instructions' page. The questions will appear on the screen in the selected language. You can also change the language of the question during the exam by selecting the desired language from the drop down list available as 'View in' on the top right of the question screen.
  7. In case of any variation in the content of the question between the English and any other language, the content in English language shall be treated as valid.
  8. One question will be displayed on the screen at a time.
  9. Time available for you to complete the examination will be displayed through a countdown timer in the top right hand corner of the screen. It will display the remaining time as Time Left. At the beginning of exam, for Part A timer will show 90 minutes which will reduce gradually with passage of time. When the timer reaches zero, the Part A examination will end by itself and then Part B exam will start automatically and the timer will show 60 minutes which will reduce gradually with passage of time. When the timer reaches zero your examination will be auto submitted by the system.
B Question Number Box:
  1. Question Number Box displayed on the right side of the screen will show the status of each question using one of the following symbols:
1 You have not visited the question yet.
2 You have not answered the question.
3 You have answered the question.
4 You have NOT answered the question, but have marked the question for review.
5 The question(s) "Answered and Marked for Review" will be considered for evaluation.
  1. The ‘Marked for Review’ status for a question simply indicates that you would like to look at that question again.
  2. You can click on the ">" arrow which appears to the left of question number box to minimise the question number box. This will enable you to view the question on a bigger area of the screen. To view the question number box again, you can click on "<" arrow which appears on the right side of the screen.
  3. You can click on to navigate to the top of the question area, without scrolling.
  4. The summary of number of questions answered, not answered, not visited, marked for review and answered and marked for review will be displayed above the question number box.
C Answering a Question
  1. The questions will appear on the screen in serial order from question number 1 to 100, which can be answered one by one as given below:

    a. To select your answer, click on the button of one of the options
    b. To deselect your chosen answer, click on the button of the chosen option again or click on the Clear Response button
    c. To change your chosen answer, click on the button of option you want to choose.
    d. To save your answer, you MUST click on the Save & Next button.
    e. In case of doubt on the option marked for a question, the question can be marked for review by clicking Mark for Review & Next button. In case the marked option is not reviewed, the originally marked option shall be considered for evaluation.
  2. To change your answer to a question that has already been answered, first click on that question number from the question number box and follow the procedure for answering as mentioned at 1 above.

D Instruction for enlarging images

To view the image provided in the question in a bigger size, click on the image and rotate the scrolling wheel on the mouse
